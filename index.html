History

Choudhary is a title of honour, usually hereditary, originating from the Indian subcontinent. It is an adaption from Sanskrit. During the Mughal rule, it was a title awarded to eminent people, while during British rule, the term was associated with zamindars and social leaders. The common female equivalent was Choudharani.

Famous People

Choudhary Devi Lal

Choudhary Devi Lal was an influential Indian politician and a prominent leader in Haryana. He served as the Deputy Prime Minister of India from 1989 to 1991. Lal played a significant role in the Indian independence movement and later in the politics of Haryana, advocating for farmers' rights and rural development. He is often remembered as "Jan Nayak" or the people's leader for his efforts in improving the lives of the common people.

Choudhary Randhir Singh

Choudhary Randhir Singh was a prominent Indian field hockey player who was part of the Indian national team that won the gold medal at the 1952 Helsinki Olympics. His contributions to Indian hockey have been highly valued, and he is remembered for his skills and dedication to the sport. Singh's legacy continues to inspire future generations of hockey players in India.
